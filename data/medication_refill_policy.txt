MEDICATION REFILL POLICY
=========================
City Health Medical Center
Document Version: 2024-05
Department: Pharmacy & Clinical Services

OVERVIEW
--------
This policy outlines the process for requesting and receiving prescription
medication refills at City Health Medical Center. Following these guidelines
ensures patient safety and compliance with applicable healthcare regulations.

HOW TO REQUEST A MEDICATION REFILL
------------------------------------
Patients may request refills through the following channels:

1. PATIENT PORTAL: Log in at myhealthportal.cityhealthmc.org and
   submit a refill request under "Medications." Requests are processed
   within 2–3 business days.

2. PHARMACY: Contact our in-house pharmacy at (800) 555-RXRX. For
   existing prescriptions at external pharmacies, ask your pharmacy to
   send an electronic refill request to your prescribing physician.

3. PHONE: Call your care team's nurse line at (800) 555-CARE and leave
   a refill request message. Include your name, date of birth, medication
   name, and pharmacy details.

4. TELEHEALTH: Medication refill requests may be reviewed and approved
   during a telehealth (virtual) visit, provided the medication is already
   prescribed and does not require an in-person physical evaluation.
   Telehealth refills are subject to physician discretion.

PROCESSING TIME
---------------
- Standard refills: 2–3 business days
- Urgent refills: Same day or next day (call the nurse line directly)
- Specialty medications: Up to 5–7 business days (may require prior
  authorization from insurance)

REFILL ELIGIBILITY REQUIREMENTS
---------------------------------
A refill request will be honored only when:
- The medication was originally prescribed by a City Health physician
  or approved provider
- The prescription is still clinically appropriate based on your
  current health status
- There are authorized refills remaining on your prescription
- Any required follow-up appointments have been completed
- Lab work (if required, e.g., for blood pressure medications, thyroid,
  diabetes drugs) is up to date

CONTROLLED SUBSTANCES
---------------------
IMPORTANT: Controlled substances (e.g., opioid pain medications,
benzodiazepines, stimulants for ADHD) are subject to STRICT regulations:

- Refills for controlled substances CANNOT be processed via the patient
  portal or phone message alone.
- An in-person or telehealth visit with your prescribing physician is
  REQUIRED before each refill of Schedule II controlled substances.
- Prescriptions for Schedule II medications (e.g., oxycodone, Adderall)
  cannot be refilled; a new prescription must be issued at each visit.
- All controlled substance prescriptions are monitored through the
  state Prescription Drug Monitoring Program (PDMP).

AUTO-REFILL PROGRAM
--------------------
Patients on long-term maintenance medications (e.g., blood pressure,
cholesterol, thyroid, diabetes medications) may enroll in our Auto-Refill
Program:
- Prescriptions are automatically refilled 7–10 days before running out
- Available for non-controlled medications only
- Requires an active prescription with refills on file
- Enroll via the patient portal or call the pharmacy

To enroll: Log into myhealthportal.cityhealthmc.org → Medications →
Enable Auto-Refill.

PRIOR AUTHORIZATION FOR REFILLS
---------------------------------
Some medications require prior authorization (approval from your insurance
before the prescription can be filled). In these cases:
- Our pharmacy team initiates the prior authorization request
- This can take 3–5 business days
- You will be notified once approved or if an alternative is needed
- Do not run out of medication while waiting — plan refills in advance

WHAT HAPPENS IF A REFILL IS DENIED?
--------------------------------------
If a refill request is denied, possible reasons include:
- No authorized refills remaining (new appointment may be required)
- Outstanding lab work or follow-up visits overdue
- Insurance prior authorization required
- Medication discontinued by your physician

Contact your care team for next steps at (800) 555-CARE.
