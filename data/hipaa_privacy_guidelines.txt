City Health Medical Center

WHAT IS HIPAA?
--------------
The Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA) is a
U.S. federal law that protects the privacy and security of your health
information. At City Health Medical Center, we are committed to
safeguarding your Protected Health Information (PHI) in compliance
with all HIPAA regulations.

WHAT IS PROTECTED HEALTH INFORMATION (PHI)?
--------------------------------------------
PHI includes any information that could identify you and relates to:
- Your past, present, or future physical or mental health condition
- Healthcare services provided to you
- Payment for healthcare services

Examples of PHI:
- Your name, address, phone number, email, or date of birth
- Medical record numbers, account numbers
- Diagnoses, medications, lab results
- Doctor's notes and treatment plans
- Images like X-rays or photographs

HOW WE USE AND SHARE YOUR INFORMATION
---------------------------------------
We may use and share your PHI for the following purposes WITHOUT
requiring your specific permission:
1. TREATMENT: Sharing information with your care team to provide
   coordinated care.
2. PAYMENT: Submitting claims to your insurance company for billing.
3. HEALTHCARE OPERATIONS: Quality reviews, training, and administrative
   functions.

We MAY share your PHI with:
- Other treating physicians involved in your care
- Laboratories, pharmacies, and imaging centers
- Insurance companies for claims processing
- Public health authorities (for disease reporting, as required by law)

We will NOT share your PHI for the following without your written
authorization:
- Marketing purposes
- Sale of your health information
- Most sharing with your employer
- Sharing with family members (unless you authorize it)

YOUR RIGHTS UNDER HIPAA
------------------------
You have the following rights regarding your health information:

1. RIGHT TO ACCESS: You may request a copy of your medical records.
   Requests are processed within 30 days. A reasonable fee may apply
   for copies. Contact the Medical Records department to request access.

2. RIGHT TO AMEND: If you believe your health information is incorrect
   or incomplete, you may request a correction. We may deny the request
   if the information was not created by us.

3. RIGHT TO ACCOUNTING OF DISCLOSURES: You may request a list of
   instances where we have shared your PHI for purposes other than
   treatment, payment, or operations.

4. RIGHT TO REQUEST RESTRICTIONS: You may ask us to limit how we use or
   share your information. We are not required to agree, but if we do,
   we must honor the restriction.

5. RIGHT TO CONFIDENTIAL COMMUNICATIONS: You may request that we contact
   you only in certain ways (e.g., call only your cell phone, not home).

6. RIGHT TO FILE A COMPLAINT: If you believe your privacy rights have
   been violated, you may file a complaint with:
   - Our Privacy Officer (see contact below), or
   - The U.S. Department of Health & Human Services (HHS) at hhs.gov/ocr

   Filing a complaint will NOT affect your care.

DATA SECURITY
-------------
We protect your PHI through:
- Encrypted electronic health records (EHR) systems
- Role-based access controls (only staff who need your data can see it)
- Regular staff training on HIPAA compliance
- Secure disposal of paper records (shredding)
- Audit logs tracking who accesses your records and when

BREACH NOTIFICATION
-------------------
If we discover a breach of your unsecured PHI, we are legally required
to notify you within 60 days of discovery. Notification will include:
- What happened
- What information was involved
- What we are doing to investigate and protect you
- What you can do to protect yourself
