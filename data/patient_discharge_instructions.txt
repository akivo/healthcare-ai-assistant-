PATIENT DISCHARGE INSTRUCTIONS
================================
City Health Medical Center
Document Version: 2024-01
Department: General Medicine & Surgery

INTRODUCTION
------------
These discharge instructions are provided to help you recover safely at home
after your hospital stay. Please read all sections carefully. If you have
any questions, contact your care team before leaving the hospital.

MEDICATIONS
-----------
You have been prescribed the following medications upon discharge. Take all
medications exactly as directed. Do not skip doses or stop medications
without consulting your doctor.

- Take all antibiotics for the full prescribed course, even if you feel better.
- Do not take more than the prescribed dose of any painkiller.
- Store all medications in a cool, dry place away from children.
- If you experience any allergic reactions (rash, swelling, difficulty
  breathing), stop the medication immediately and call 911 or go to the
  nearest emergency room.

WOUND CARE (if applicable)
---------------------------
- Keep the wound clean and dry for the first 48 hours.
- Change the dressing as instructed by your nurse, typically once daily.
- Do not submerge the wound in water (no baths, swimming) until cleared by
  your doctor.
- Watch for signs of infection: increasing redness, warmth, swelling,
  pus/discharge, or fever above 101°F (38.3°C).
- Do not remove stitches or staples yourself. Your doctor will remove them
  at your follow-up appointment.

ACTIVITY RESTRICTIONS
---------------------
- Rest for the first 24-48 hours after discharge.
- Avoid heavy lifting (more than 10 lbs) for at least 2 weeks unless
  otherwise instructed.
- Do not drive for 24 hours after receiving anesthesia or sedation.
- Gradually return to normal activities as tolerated.
- Avoid strenuous exercise until your follow-up appointment.

DIET AND HYDRATION
------------------
- Drink at least 8 glasses of water per day unless your doctor has
  restricted fluid intake.
- Follow any special dietary instructions given by your care team (low-sodium,
  low-fat, diabetic diet, etc.).
- Avoid alcohol for at least 48 hours, and while taking any prescription
  medications.
- Eat small, frequent meals if you experience nausea.

FOLLOW-UP APPOINTMENT
---------------------
You MUST schedule and attend your follow-up appointment within 7-14 days
of discharge. Failure to attend follow-up appointments can result in
complications that require re-hospitalization.

To schedule: Call our scheduling line at (800) 555-CARE or visit
the patient portal at myhealthportal.cityhealthmc.org.

WARNING SIGNS — SEEK EMERGENCY CARE IMMEDIATELY
-------------------------------------------------
Go to the nearest emergency room or call 911 if you experience:
- Chest pain or difficulty breathing
- Sudden severe headache
- High fever above 103°F (39.4°C)
- Uncontrolled bleeding
- Confusion or loss of consciousness
- Swelling, redness, or pain in the legs (possible blood clot)
- Sudden vision changes
