INSURANCE ELIGIBILITY — FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS (FAQ)
=========================================================
City Health Medical Center
Document Version: 2024-03
Department: Patient Financial Services

GENERAL QUESTIONS
-----------------

Q: What insurance plans does City Health Medical Center accept?
A: We accept most major insurance plans including:
   - Blue Cross Blue Shield (all plans)
   - Aetna (HMO, PPO, EPO)
   - UnitedHealthcare (all plans)
   - Cigna (HMO, PPO)
   - Humana (HMO, PPO, Medicare Advantage)
   - Medicare (Parts A & B)
   - Medicaid (state-approved plans)
   - Tricare (military)
   - Oscar Health
   For a complete list, visit our website or call Patient Financial Services.

Q: How do I find out if my insurance covers a specific procedure?
A: Contact your insurance provider directly using the member services number
   on the back of your insurance card. You can also call our Patient Financial
   Services team at (800) 555-BILL and we will help verify your benefits
   before the appointment.

Q: What is a referral and do I need one?
A: A referral is an authorization from your primary care physician (PCP)
   to see a specialist. Whether you need one depends on your insurance plan:
   - HMO plans: Usually require a referral for specialist visits.
   - PPO plans: Referrals are typically NOT required.
   - If unsure, call your insurance company before scheduling.

Q: What is pre-authorization?
A: Pre-authorization (also called prior authorization) is when your insurance
   company must approve certain procedures, tests, or medications before they
   are performed. Common examples include MRI scans, surgeries, and specialty
   drugs. Our clinical team handles pre-authorization requests on your behalf,
   but we recommend allowing 3–5 business days for approval.

COSTS & BILLING
---------------

Q: What is a copay?
A: A copay is a fixed amount you pay at the time of service regardless of
   the total bill. For example, $20 for a primary care visit or $50 for
   a specialist visit. Copay amounts are set by your insurance plan.

Q: What is a deductible?
A: A deductible is the amount you must pay out-of-pocket each year before
   your insurance starts covering costs. For example, if your deductible is
   $1,000, you pay the first $1,000 of medical expenses yourself each year.

Q: What is coinsurance?
A: Coinsurance is the percentage of costs you share with your insurance
   after your deductible is met. For example, if your coinsurance is 20%
   and the bill is $500, you pay $100 and insurance pays $400.

Q: What if I don't have insurance?
A: City Health Medical Center offers a financial assistance program
   (charity care) for eligible uninsured or underinsured patients.
   Contact Patient Financial Services to apply. We also offer
   self-pay discounts and payment plans.

Q: Will I receive a bill if I have insurance?
A: Yes, after your insurance processes the claim, you will receive an
   Explanation of Benefits (EOB) from your insurer and a bill from us
   for any remaining balance (deductible, copay, coinsurance).

OUT-OF-NETWORK
--------------

Q: What does out-of-network mean?
A: A provider or facility is out-of-network when they don't have a
   contract with your insurance plan. This usually means higher costs
   for you. City Health Medical Center is IN-NETWORK with most major
   plans, but individual doctors may have different network status.

Q: Can I still be treated if I'm out-of-network?
A: Yes, but you will typically pay more. For emergencies, federal law
   protects you from surprise billing — you can only be charged
   in-network cost-sharing rates for emergency services.

CLAIM ISSUES
------------

Q: My claim was denied. What should I do?
A: First, review the denial reason on your EOB. You have the right to
   appeal insurance denials. Our Patient Advocacy team can assist you
   with the appeals process. Call (800) 555-BILL within 30 days of
   receiving a denial notice.

Q: How long does it take for a claim to be processed?
A: Most claims are processed within 30–45 days of the service date.
   Complex claims or those requiring pre-authorization may take longer.
