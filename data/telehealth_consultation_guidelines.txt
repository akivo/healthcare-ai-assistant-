City Health Medical Center

WHAT IS TELEHEALTH?
--------------------
Telehealth (also called telemedicine or virtual care) allows patients
to consult with their healthcare provider using video, phone, or
secure messaging — without visiting the clinic in person.
City Health Medical Center's telehealth platform provides secure,
HIPAA-compliant virtual consultations.

WHO IS ELIGIBLE FOR TELEHEALTH VISITS?
----------------------------------------
Telehealth services are available to:
- Established patients of City Health Medical Center
- New patients for general medicine consultations (specialty referrals
  may still require in-person evaluation first)
- Patients with stable, chronic conditions for routine follow-ups
- Patients seeking non-emergency acute care (cold, flu, rash, UTI, etc.)
- Patients requesting medication refills (see Medication Refill Policy)
- Patients requiring mental health or counseling sessions

Telehealth is NOT appropriate for:
- Medical emergencies (call 911)
- Conditions requiring physical examination (e.g., abdominal pain,
  orthopedic injuries, cardiac evaluation)
- Procedures, injections, or lab work
- First-time consultations for complex or undiagnosed conditions
  (at provider discretion)

TECHNOLOGY REQUIREMENTS
------------------------
To participate in a telehealth visit, you will need:
- A smartphone, tablet, or computer with a working camera and microphone
- A stable internet connection (minimum 5 Mbps recommended)
- The CityHealth Patient mobile app (iOS/Android) or a modern web browser
  (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge)

SCHEDULING A TELEHEALTH APPOINTMENT
--------------------------------------
1. Log in to the patient portal at myhealthportal.cityhealthmc.org
2. Select "Schedule Appointment" → Choose "Telehealth/Virtual Visit"
3. Select department and preferred date/time
4. You will receive a secure video link via email and SMS
5. Click the link 5 minutes before your appointment time to join

Same-day telehealth appointments may be available — call (800) 555-CARE
to check availability.

WHAT TO EXPECT DURING YOUR VIRTUAL VISIT
------------------------------------------
- Your visit will begin within 5–10 minutes of the scheduled time
- Have your insurance card, photo ID, and medication list ready
- The provider may ask you to show symptoms via camera (rash, swelling, etc.)
- You will receive a visit summary and any new prescriptions via the portal
- Follow-up appointments will be scheduled if needed

PRESCRIPTIONS AND MEDICATION REFILLS VIA TELEHEALTH
------------------------------------------------------
Providers may prescribe or refill certain medications during telehealth visits:

ALLOWED via Telehealth:
- Refills for existing, stable medications (blood pressure, diabetes,
  thyroid, cholesterol, etc.)
- New prescriptions for common conditions (antibiotics, antihistamines,
  anti-inflammatory medications)
- Mental health medications (for established patients under psychiatric care)

NOT ALLOWED via Telehealth:
- Schedule II controlled substances (opioids, certain ADHD medications)
  — these require in-person evaluation
- Medications requiring physical examination before prescribing
- New controlled substance prescriptions for new patients

INSURANCE AND COVERAGE
-----------------------
Most major insurance plans now cover telehealth visits, especially
post-COVID-19 legislative changes. Coverage may vary by plan:
- Medicare: Covers telehealth for most services
- Medicaid: Coverage varies by state — contact your plan
- Commercial Insurance: Most plans cover telehealth at the same
  copay as in-person visits

Call your insurance provider to confirm telehealth coverage before
your appointment. Self-pay rates are available — contact Patient
Financial Services at (800) 555-BILL.

PRIVACY AND SECURITY
---------------------
All telehealth visits are conducted over a HIPAA-compliant, encrypted
platform. Your visit is private and secure. We recommend:
- Joining from a private, quiet location
- Using a personal device (not shared/public)
- Using a private Wi-Fi network, not public Wi-Fi
- Not recording the session without provider consent

TECHNICAL DIFFICULTIES
-----------------------
If you experience technical issues during your visit:
- Try refreshing the browser or restarting the app
- Check your internet connection
- If the video fails, the provider may switch to a phone call
- Contact technical support: techsupport@cityhealthmc.org or
  call (800) 555-TECH

CANCELLATION POLICY
-------------------
Telehealth appointments follow the same cancellation policy as in-person
visits. Please cancel at least 24 hours in advance to avoid a no-show fee.
